Bayesian Product Discovery

A small static demo for showing how a product idea moves from a prior belief, through problem evidence, then through delivery capability evidence.

Prior
20%
After problem evidence
94%
Delivery capability
70%
Overall belief
66%

What this chart is showing

Start with an outside-view prior: the chance this project will produce the intended outcome before we run discovery. Then add problem evidence: five interviews where people independently cite the same problem as important enough to solve. Then add delivery capability: the chance the team can get a thin slice into a production environment within four weeks.

The final number is deliberately simple: the problem-evidence posterior multiplied by delivery capability. It says: even if the problem looks real, confidence should be discounted if we are not yet confident we can deliver a real slice.

overall belief = P(success after problem evidence) × P(deliver a production slice in 4 weeks)

The two levels of inference

Level Question What updates
1. Problem discovery Do users cite this as a problem important to them? Our belief that the idea is worth pursuing.
2. Delivery capability Can the team deliver a thin slice to production within four weeks? Our belief that the opportunity can become real quickly enough to matter.

Why five interviews can matter

Five interviews do not prove universal truth. They reduce uncertainty. If five people are sampled independently and all five point to the same important problem, that is a stronger signal than vague stakeholder enthusiasm or dashboard activity.

The point is not to pretend the number is perfect. The point is to make belief visible, testable, and updateable.

How to use it in a workshop

Ask the group for the prior first, before discussing the new evidence. Then ask what the five interviews should do to belief. Finally, ask whether delivery capability should increase or discount confidence. The conversation usually exposes whether people are arguing about the problem, the solution, or the organisation’s ability to ship.
